Support services for people leaving prison

Part of the Prison and detention topic

Find government and other support services for people transitioning from life in prison and reintegrating into the community.

Queensland Corrective Services

Provides reintegration and support programs for people leaving prison.

Services include

  • Offending behaviour programs
  • Vocational education and training programs
  • Prison industries
  • Pastoral care
  • Cultural support programs
  • Transitional support

Queensland Corrective Services website

Phone: 13 74 68

  • Queensland Government

Community Re-entry Support Team (CREST)

Provides community support services to people transitioning from prison and reintegrating.

Services include

  • Short-term transitional support, including referrals for housing, medical treatment, and other support.
  • Human case management support, including meeting with people and a network of support to support transition back into the community.
  • Holistic support services, including assistance for people entering or staying in the community as requested by community corrections officers.

Community Re-entry Support Team website

  • Queensland Government

Borallon Training and Correctional Centre (BTCC)

Provides support services to help people in training and correctional programs transition back into the community.

Services include

  • Training to help people develop skills and address needs they may have for their reintegration.
  • Vocational training and development to provide pathways to employment opportunities.

Borallon Training and Correctional Centre website

  • Queensland Government

Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages

Provides services to help people obtain identity documents and certificates required for employment, housing, and government services.

Registry of Births, Deaths and Marriages website

Phone: 13 74 68

Email: bdm-mail@justice.qld.gov.au

  • Australian Government

Centrelink (Services Australia)

Provides financial assistance and support for people leaving prison.

Services include

  • Crisis payment
  • Other payments depending on eligibility

Centrelink website

Find a Centrelink service centre near you

Phone: 13 24 68

  • Not-for-profit organisation

Brisbane Common Ground

Offers housing for people who have experienced homelessness and those at risk of homelessness.

Services include

  • Well-managed housing support for reintegration into the community.

Brisbane Common Ground website

Phone: 07 3370 4700

Email: info@common-ground.org.au

  • Not-for-profit organisation

Centacare Brisbane

Provides support for people who have been in prison.

Services include

  • Case management and referrals to support services including transitional housing, medical assistance, and other support.

Centacare Brisbane website

Phone: 07 3324 3100

Email: centacare@bne.catholic.net.au

  • Not-for-profit organisation

MARA – Southeast Queensland Women's Re-entry Services

Gender-specific support for women exiting correctional centres in Queensland. Support starts in prison and continues for up to 12 months post-release.

Services include

  • Referral to services specific to women, including domestic violence and health services.
  • Holistic case management identifying individual needs such as health, mental health, drug and alcohol support, education, training, housing, and family support.

MARA – Southeast Queensland Women's Re-entry Services website

  • Not-for-profit organisation

Prisoners' Legal Service

Provides free legal advice and assistance to prisoners in Queensland.

Services include

  • Legal matters related to incarceration.
  • Post-release challenges.

Prisoners' Legal Service website

  • Not-for-profit organisation

Prison Ministry of the Uniting Church

Supports people in correctional centres through pastoral care and community support, helping navigate the transition to the community.

Prison Ministry of the Uniting Church website
